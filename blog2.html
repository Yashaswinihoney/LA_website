Perfection in Imperfection

LoveActuallyME !

“You have no idea how nasty and cruel this ruthless society is.” I heard the doctor say to my parents while I was busy fiddling in my bed. They refused to believe their son was any less and were sure he was brilliant. Yes, that’s me, I'm just born six days diagnosed with cerebral palsy (CP).

To those who are still wondering what cerebral palsy is like, CP is a condition that affects the coordination between mind and body. From that day to now every day has been a challenge for me, and it’s something which has become a part and parcel of my life, trust me there is no greater joy in doing things that people say you can’t. For nearly eight years, I stayed at home with no friends or schoolmates until I joined a society working for PWDs.

I learned typing with one finger and had a blooming love for numbers. After 8th grade, I joined a regular school, which I would say was the turning point as I had to now stay amongst regular kids. Mockery and long unusual stares became prominent and routine for me, but I was just glad that I could influence and make them learn about CP through me. Maths and numbers come naturally to me, so I took commerce in 12th boards and scored decently. I felt like nothing could stop me, and no one would doubt my intelligence. But I was turned away by many colleges due to my condition.

I could not pursue what I desire for off my disability for which even I’m not responsible. Everyone told me doing Masters is too ambitious. I not only applied for the Masters but also secured a distinction slapping the stereotypes along with the grades.

So, here I work in a bank, still ignoring the petty mockeries. I would just bid adieu by stating that disability is not our inheritance but how we tackle and grow! I see the world very differently, where society has nothing to do with my success or failure. Only I am responsible for where my life goes and believe that change begins with me. Having a disability is not easy in a society that is majorly ignorant and insensitive. There are all kinds of people, some mock you, and some support you unconditionally, but that in no way should affect me.
