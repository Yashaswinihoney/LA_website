What 2020 taught us

For better or worse, 2020 has certainly changed us. And now, looking back, one can see the lessons we’ve learned about health, resilience, and community. We learned to be appreciative of our health and even more concerned about the health of our planet.

We’ve learned how to surrender with grace, whether we wanted to or not. The year 2020 was an adventurous ride due to Covid courtesy China but gave us opportunities to slow down, pause, and revisit ourselves. It was a testing period for and taught us how important it is to live in the moment.

Perfection in Imperfection

“You have no idea how nasty and cruel this ruthless society is.” I heard the doctor say to my parents while I was busy fiddling in my bed. They refused to believe their son was any less and were sure he was brilliant. Yes, that’s me, I'm just born six days diagnosed with cerebral palsy (CP).

To those who are still wondering what cerebral palsy is like, CP is a condition that affects the coordination between mind and body. From that day to now every day has been a challenge for me, and it’s something which has become a part and parcel of my life, trust me there is no greater joy in doing things that people say you can’t. For nearly eight years, I stayed at home with no friends or schoolmates until I joined a society working for PWDs.
